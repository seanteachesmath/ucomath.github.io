39.

The period of a pendulum is the time it takes for the pendulum to complete one entire swing, from left to right and back again. The greater the length, \(L\text{,}\) of the pendulum, the longer its period, \(T\text{.}\) In fact, if \(L\) is measured in feet, then the period is given in seconds by

\begin{equation*} T=2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{32}} \end{equation*}

  1. Write the formula for \(T\) as a power function in the form \(f (x) = kx^p\text{.}\)

  2. Suppose you are standing in the Convention Center in Portland, Oregon, and you time the period of its Foucault pendulum (the longest in the world). Its period is approximately \(10.54\) seconds. How long is the pendulum?

  3. Choose a reasonable domain for the function \(T = f (L)\) and graph the function.

Answer

  1. \(T=\dfrac{2\pi}{\sqrt{32}}L^{1/2} \)

  2. \(90\) feet

41.

If you walk in the normal way, your maximum speed, \(v\text{,}\) in meters per second, is limited by the length of your legs, \(r\text{,}\) according to the formula

\begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{gr} \end{equation*}

where the constant \(g\) is approximately \(10\) meters per second squared. (Source: Alexander, 1992)

  1. A typical adult man has legs about \(0.9\) meter long. How fast can he walk?

  2. A typical four-year-old has legs \(0.5\) meter long. How fast can she walk?

  3. Graph maximum walking speed as a function of leg length.

  4. Race-walkers can walk as fast as \(4.4\) meters per second by rotating their hips so that the effective length of their legs is increased. What is that effective length?

  5. On the Moon the value of \(g\) is \(1.6\) meters per second squared. How fast can a typical adult man walk on the Moon?

Answer

  1. \(3\) meters per second

  2. \(b\approx 2.2\) meters per second

  4. \(1.9\) meters

  5. \(1.2\)meters per second

When a ship moves through the water, it creates waves that impede its own progress. Because of this resistance, there is an upper limit to the speed at which a ship can travel, given, in knots, by

\begin{equation*} v_{\text{max}}=1.3\sqrt{L} \end{equation*}

where \(L\) is the length of the vessel, in feet. (Source: Gilner, 1972)

  1. Graph maximum speed as a function of vessel length.

  2. The world's largest ship, the oil tanker Jahre Viking, is \(1054\) feet long. What is its top speed?

  3. As a ship approaches its maximum speed, the power required increases sharply. Therefore, most merchant ships are designed to cruise at speeds no higher than \(v_c = 0.8\sqrt{L}\text{.}\) Graph \(v_c\) on the same axes with \(v_\text{max}\text{.}\)

  4. What is the cruising speed of the Jahre Viking? What percent of its maximum speed is that?
